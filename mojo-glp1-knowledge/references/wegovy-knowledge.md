# Wegovy Knowledge Base

> Note: This document summarises the approved uses, dose forms, and safety profile of Wegovy. **It is reference material only, not medical advice.** Any decision involving dose adjustments, injection technique, storage, or side-effect management should be confirmed with a prescribing physician or pharmacist.

Sources: FDA Prescribing Information (DailyMed), EMA Product Information, Novo Nordisk manufacturer resources, STEP clinical trial program, r/WegovyWeightLoss community pattern summaries.

---

## Core facts

- **Manufacturer**: Novo Nordisk
- **FDA approval**: 2021 (chronic weight management in adults and adolescents ≥12 years with obesity or overweight with weight-related comorbidities); 2024 expansion for MACE reduction in adults with established cardiovascular disease; additional indication for noncirrhotic MASH (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis) with moderate to advanced fibrosis
- **EMA approval**: 2021 (weight management in adults; adolescent approval followed)
- **TFDA (Taiwan)**: Limited public information; not routinely available in Taiwan retail market
- **Generic name**: semaglutide (司美格魯肽 / 司美格鲁肽), GLP-1 receptor agonist
- **Approved indications**: Chronic weight management in adults and adolescents ≥12 years with obesity (BMI ≥30) or overweight (BMI ≥27 with weight-related comorbidity such as hypertension, type 2 diabetes, or cardiovascular disease); reduction of MACE in adults with established CVD and overweight or obesity; noncirrhotic MASH with moderate to advanced fibrosis (recent)
- **Form**: Single-dose pre-filled pens (once-weekly injections); single-dose syringes in select markets
- **Standard titration**: 0.25 mg once weekly for 4 weeks (initiation, not weight-loss effective) → 0.5 mg → 1 mg → 1.7 mg → 2.4 mg maintenance over 16+ weeks. Some markets offer a higher-dose 7.2 mg pen; escalation occurs at prescriber discretion if 2.4 mg is not tolerated or response is inadequate.
- **Dosing frequency**: Once weekly, subcutaneous injection (皮下注射)
- **Half-life**: Approximately 7 days; ~5 weeks to clear from circulation
- **Storage**: Unopened: 2–8°C (refrigerate; do not freeze). After first use: 15–30°C or refrigerated, maximum **28 days in original carton**. Protect from light.

## Common community misconceptions

### Misconception 1: "Wegovy is a quick-fix miracle shot; you don't need diet or exercise"

**Incorrect.** Wegovy is labelled as an **adjunct** to reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity. Clinical trial weight loss (STEP program) ranges from 15–18% of baseline body weight at 2.4 mg maintenance, and placebo-controlled studies show that diet + exercise + Wegovy yields significantly greater loss than any single component alone. Discontinuing diet/exercise improvements while using Wegovy results in suboptimal weight loss and faster regain after stopping.

### Misconception 2: "Once you stop Wegovy, weight stays off permanently"

**Incorrect.** Wegovy is designed for *chronic* use under ongoing medical supervision. Clinical extension trials (STEP 1 continuation, STEP 4) show that weight regain begins after discontinuation and returns to near-baseline within 1–2 years in most users who do not maintain diet/exercise changes. Wegovy is a long-term management tool, not a one-time treatment. Restarting after a gap requires re-titration from 0.25 mg.

### Misconception 3: "Wegovy is just Ozempic rebranded for weight loss"

**Partially correct but misleading.** Wegovy and Ozempic both contain semaglutide (司美格魯肽 / 司美格鲁肽), a GLP-1 agonist. However, they are distinct products: **Ozempic** is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes (maximum 2 mg weekly) and is not labelled for weight management. **Wegovy** is approved for chronic weight management with a higher maintenance dose (2.4 mg; up to 7.2 mg in some markets). The two are not interchangeable—Ozempic lacks the weight-management indication, and using it for weight loss is off-label. Dosing, packaging (single-dose pens vs multi-dose), and intended patient populations differ.

## Use and storage essentials

- **Injection sites**: Subcutaneous injection (皮下注射) into abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Rotate injection sites between weekly doses to avoid lipohypertrophy.
- **New needle every injection**: Use a fresh sterile needle for each dose; needles are single-use.
- **Single-dose pen; discard after use**: Each Wegovy pen contains one week's dose and is discarded immediately after injection. Do not attempt to reuse.
- **Timing between doses**: Administer once every 7 days (±2–3 days acceptable; prescriber may allow shift to a different day of the week for convenience).
- **Missed dose protocol**:
  - If ≤2 days late: inject as soon as possible; return to regular schedule the following week.
  - If >2 days late: skip that dose and resume regular schedule next week.
  - Do not double-dose.
- **Storage after removal from refrigerator**: 15–30°C or refrigerated in original carton; **maximum 28 days**. Once the 28-day window closes, discard unused pens.
- **Inspection**: Solution should be clear and colorless to slightly opalescent. Do not use if cloudy, discoloured, or contains particles.

## Common side effects

### Very common (>20%)
- **Nausea (噁心 / 恶心)**: Most frequent early side effect; onset within first 1–2 weeks. Usually mild–moderate; improves by weeks 4–8. Slower titration reduces intensity.
- **Diarrhea (腹瀉 / 腹泻)**: Ranges from loose stools to increased frequency. Usually transient; often resolves by week 8–12.
- **Vomiting (嘔吐 / 呕吐)**: Less common than nausea; typically mild and short-lived.
- **Constipation (便秘)**: Paradoxically reported alongside diarrhea; varies by individual.
- **Abdominal pain / distension**: Usually mild; associated with GI effects.
- **Fatigue (疲勞 / 疲乏)**: Reported in some users; often improves with time.
- **Headache**: Mild; may occur during titration.
- **Injection site reactions**: Redness, bruising, mild swelling; usually resolves within hours.
- **Increased heart rate (tachycardia)**: Documented in trials; usually mild; normalizes with continued treatment.
- **Hair loss (alopecia)**: Reported in weight-loss cohorts; typically temporary and associated with rapid weight loss.

### Common (5–20%)
- **Decreased appetite (食慾不振 / 食欲不振)**: Intentional mechanism; contributes to weight loss.
- **Dizziness**: Usually mild; often early-phase.

### Uncommon/Serious (require medical evaluation)
- **Acute pancreatitis**: Rare; presents with severe upper abdominal pain radiating to back, nausea, vomiting. Seek emergency care.
- **Gallbladder events** (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis): Reported in weight-loss populations; risk increases with rapid weight loss. Report right-sided abdominal pain or jaundice.
- **Acute kidney injury (AKI)**: Associated with dehydration from vomiting/diarrhea. Maintain hydration; report decreased urine output or elevated creatinine.
- **Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)**: Risk is low in non-diabetic users; risk increases if used concurrently with insulin or SU.
- **Retinopathy complications**: In users with pre-existing diabetic retinopathy, vision changes or floaters should prompt urgent ophthalmology review.
- **Allergic reactions**: Rash, hives, urticaria; rare anaphylaxis. Discontinue and seek immediate care if anaphylaxis suspected.

### **Boxed warning (most serious)**:
- **Thyroid C-cell tumors (medullary thyroid carcinoma, MTC)**: Animal studies showed dose-related C-cell proliferation. Contraindicated in personal or family history of MTC or MEN 2 syndrome.

## Drug interactions and contraindications

### Contraindications
- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Known hypersensitivity to semaglutide or any ingredient

### Major interactions
- **Insulin or sulfonylureas**: Concurrent use increases hypoglycemia risk. Dose adjustment of insulin/SU may be necessary. Monitor blood glucose.
- **Oral contraceptives**: Delayed gastric emptying may transiently reduce absorption during initiation or escalation phases. FDA guidance suggests considering barrier contraception during dose titration; discuss with prescriber if breakthrough bleeding occurs.
- **Warfarin / coumarins**: No major INR change in formal studies, but **decreased INR reported with acenocoumarol in some cases**. INR monitoring recommended when starting or changing Wegovy dose.
- **Oral medications**: Delayed gastric emptying affects absorption of certain medications, particularly those with narrow absorption windows (e.g. levothyroxine, antibiotics). Spacing doses and monitoring therapeutic levels advised.
- **Alcohol**: Caution recommended; may increase hypoglycemia risk if used with insulin or SU.

## Residual medication

**Not applicable.** Wegovy is supplied as single-dose pre-filled pens. After each injection, the pen is discarded. There is no multi-dose mechanism or residual-use scenario.

## Lifestyle considerations during treatment

- **Diet and exercise**: Weight loss is optimized when combined with a reduced-calorie diet (~500 kcal/day deficit) and regular physical activity. Nutrition and fitness consultation recommended, especially for users planning long-term use.
- **Hydration**: Adequate fluid intake mitigates GI symptoms and reduces AKI risk, particularly during the first weeks of treatment.
- **Protein intake**: Dietary protein (1.2–1.6 g/kg body weight daily) helps preserve lean muscle mass during weight loss.
- **Alcohol**: Caution advised; may impair blood glucose regulation and increase hypoglycemia risk if used with insulin/SU.
- **Pregnancy**: Semaglutide is not studied in pregnancy and should be discontinued ≥2 months before attempting conception. If pregnancy is confirmed during use, consult prescriber immediately.
- **Fertility**: Limited data on effects on ovulation or male fertility. Discuss family-planning timelines with prescriber.
- **Going on/off**: Do not stop abruptly. Gradual de-escalation is typically recommended. Re-titration from 0.25 mg is standard if restarting after a gap.
- **Concurrent GI medications**: If using Wegovy alongside metformin, acarbose, or other GI-active agents, monitor for compounded effects and consult prescriber.
- **Common OTC drugs**: Most OTC medications are compatible; antacids may affect absorption of some compounds. Consult pharmacist if concerns.
