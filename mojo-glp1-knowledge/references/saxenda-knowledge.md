# Saxenda (善纖達) Knowledge Base

> Note: This document summarises the approved uses, dose forms, and safety profile of Saxenda. **It is reference material only, not medical advice.** Any decision involving dose adjustments, injection technique, storage, or side-effect management should be confirmed with a prescribing physician or pharmacist.

Sources: FDA Prescribing Information (DailyMed), EMA Product Information, Novo Nordisk manufacturer resources, SCALE clinical trial program, r/Saxenda community pattern summaries.

---

## Core facts

- **Manufacturer**: Novo Nordisk
- **FDA approval**: 2014 (chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight with weight-related comorbidities); 2020 expansion to adolescents ≥12 years with obesity (body weight >60 kg)
- **EMA approval**: 2015 (weight management in adults and adolescents ≥12 years)
- **TFDA (Taiwan)**: Approved; product name 善纖達 injection, indicated for weight management (license 衛部藥輸字第 025769 號)
- **Generic name**: liraglutide (利拉魯肽 / 利拉鲁肽), GLP-1 receptor agonist
- **Approved indications**: Chronic weight management in adults with obesity (BMI ≥30) or overweight (BMI ≥27 with weight-related comorbidities); pediatric use ≥12 years with obesity (body weight >60 kg). **Not indicated for type 2 diabetes** (Victoza is the T2D-approved form of liraglutide; Saxenda is weight-management only).
- **Form**: Pre-filled multi-dose pens (dial-a-dose); single-patient use only
- **Standard titration**: 0.6 mg once daily for 1 week, then increase by 0.6 mg weekly: 0.6 mg → 1.2 mg → 1.8 mg → 2.4 mg → 3.0 mg (maintenance from week 5). Escalation may be delayed or a lower maintenance dose used if not tolerated. Pediatric escalation may extend up to 8 weeks.
- **Dosing frequency**: **Once DAILY** (differs from weekly agents: Ozempic, Wegovy, Zepbound, Mounjaro)
- **Half-life**: Approximately 13 hours (much shorter than weekly agents); accounts for daily administration requirement
- **Storage**: Unopened: 2–8°C (refrigerate; do not freeze). After first use: 15–30°C or refrigerated, maximum **30 days**. Protect from light.

## Common community misconceptions

### Misconception 1: "Saxenda is injected once weekly like Ozempic or Wegovy"

**Incorrect.** Saxenda is administered **once DAILY**, not weekly. This critical difference is due to liraglutide's much shorter half-life (~13 hours vs ~7 days for semaglutide). Daily injection is more demanding than weekly; users must incorporate this into their daily routine. Missing doses has more immediate impact on blood levels than missing a weekly injection. This is a major factor in medication adherence and user experience.

### Misconception 2: "Saxenda has fewer GI side effects than newer GLP-1s like semaglutide"

**Incorrect.** Saxenda produces GI side effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation) at rates comparable to or higher than newer agents. Common side effects occur in >20% of users in SCALE trials. Daily administration means more frequent drug exposure per week (7 doses vs 1), which may make GI effects feel more persistent or pronounced in community reports. The burden of daily injection plus frequent early GI symptoms contributes to higher discontinuation rates compared to weekly agents in some populations.

### Misconception 3: "Weight lost on Saxenda stays off after you stop taking it"

**Incorrect.** Saxenda is designed for *chronic* use under medical supervision. Clinical extension trials (SCALE Maintenance) show that weight regain begins within weeks of discontinuation and returns to near-baseline within 1–2 years if diet/exercise do not improve. Saxenda is a long-term management tool, not a one-time treatment. Re-titration from 0.6 mg is standard if restarting after a gap.

## Use and storage essentials

- **Injection sites**: Subcutaneous injection (皮下注射) into abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Rotate injection sites daily or between daily injections to avoid lipohypertrophy.
- **New needle every injection**: Use a fresh sterile needle for each daily dose; needles are single-use.
- **Dial-a-dose pen**: Before each injection, rotate the dial to the desired dose (0.6 / 1.2 / 1.8 / 2.4 / 3.0 mg). Audible or tactile click signals correct dosing.
- **Timing**: Administer once daily, preferably at the same time each day (e.g. morning or evening). Timing relative to meals is flexible (unlike Rybelsus).
- **Missed dose protocol**:
  - If you miss a daily dose, skip it and resume your regular once-daily schedule the next day.
  - Do not double-dose to make up.
  - Missing one or two doses may briefly reduce drug level but does not require re-titration.
- **Storage after opening**: 15–30°C or refrigerated in original carton; **maximum 30 days**. Once opened, discard the pen after 30 days or when empty, whichever is first.
- **Inspection**: Solution should be clear and colorless. Do not use if cloudy, discoloured, or contains particles.

## Common side effects

### Very common (>20% in SCALE trials)
- **Nausea (噁心 / 恶心)**: Most frequent early side effect; onset within first week of starting or dose escalation. Usually mild–moderate; improves by weeks 4–8 in many users. Slower titration reduces intensity.
- **Vomiting (嘔吐 / 呕吐)**: Common; ranges from rare to frequent depending on individual tolerance. Usually most pronounced during titration.
- **Diarrhea (腹瀉 / 腹泻)**: Ranges from loose stools to more frequent bowel movements. Usually transient; improves by week 8–12.
- **Constipation (便秘)**: Paradoxically reported alongside diarrhea in some users.
- **Abdominal pain / distension**: Often accompanies GI effects; mild–moderate.
- **Decreased appetite (食慾不振 / 食欲不振)**: Intentional mechanism; contributes to weight loss.
- **Headache**: Mild; early-phase.
- **Fatigue (疲勞 / 疲乏)**: Reported in some users; often improves with time.
- **Injection site reactions**: Redness, swelling, bruising, or itching at injection site; usually mild and transient.
- **Increased heart rate (tachycardia)**: Documented; usually mild; normalizes with continued treatment.
- **Dizziness**: Reported; usually mild and early-phase.

### Common (5–20%)
- **Flu-like symptoms**: Fever, chills, malaise; reported in small percentage, usually early.

### Uncommon/Serious (require medical evaluation)
- **Acute pancreatitis**: Rare; presents with severe upper abdominal pain radiating to back, nausea, vomiting. Seek emergency care immediately.
- **Gallbladder events** (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis): Reported in weight-loss populations; risk increases with rapid weight loss. Report right-sided abdominal pain, jaundice, or fever.
- **Acute kidney injury (AKI)**: Associated with dehydration from vomiting/diarrhea. Maintain hydration; report decreased urine output.
- **Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)**: Risk is low in non-obese non-diabetic users; risk increases if used concurrently with insulin or sulfonylureas (though not approved for diabetics, off-label combined use may occur).
- **Allergic reactions**: Rash, hives, urticaria; rare anaphylaxis. Discontinue and seek immediate care.

### **Boxed warning (most serious)**:
- **Thyroid C-cell tumors (medullary thyroid carcinoma, MTC)**: Animal studies showed dose-related C-cell proliferation. Contraindicated in personal or family history of MTC or MEN 2 syndrome.

## Drug interactions and contraindications

### Contraindications
- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Known hypersensitivity to liraglutide or any ingredient

### Major interactions
- **Insulin or sulfonylureas**: Concurrent use increases hypoglycemia risk (though Saxenda is not approved for diabetics, off-label combined use may occur). Dose reduction may be necessary. Monitor blood glucose closely.
- **Oral medications**: Delayed gastric emptying may affect absorption of some oral drugs, particularly those requiring rapid GI absorption. Spacing doses and monitoring therapeutic levels advised.
- **Warfarin / coumarins**: No major INR change in formal studies. INR monitoring recommended if concurrent use.
- **Alcohol**: Caution advised; may impair glucose regulation.

## Residual medication (multi-dose pen)

### The facts
- The Saxenda pen is designed to deliver 6 doses at 3.0 mg maintenance; fluid remains only if dialing down before completion.
- **Manufacturer's instruction**: Discard the pen 30 days after first use or when empty, whichever is first.
- Community discussions (r/Saxenda, off-label "extend supply" threads) describe users attempting to extend pen life or dial smaller increments to stretch supply. **Any deviation from manufacturer instructions carries risks.**

### Why extension is risky
1. **Sterility loss**: The dial mechanism is not designed for extended operation; repeated use beyond 30 days or past dial failure increases bacterial contamination risk.
2. **Dosing inaccuracy**: After 30 days, liraglutide formulation stability is not guaranteed; dose precision may degrade.
3. **Shelf-life uncertainty**: The 30-day window applies to manufacturer conditions; extended storage lacks validated safety data.

### What only a clinician should decide
- Whether to consider pen extension or dosing adjustments in individual cases.

An informational document should not provide step-by-step extension instructions; that is a medical decision requiring prescriber oversight.

## Lifestyle considerations during treatment

- **Daily injection commitment**: Daily administration (not weekly) requires sustained adherence; missed doses accumulate faster than weekly agents. Building a consistent daily routine is crucial.
- **Hydration**: Adequate fluid intake, especially during early GI side-effect phase. Dehydration increases AKI risk.
- **Protein intake**: Dietary protein (1.2–1.6 g/kg body weight daily) helps preserve lean muscle mass during weight loss.
- **Diet and exercise**: Weight loss optimizes when combined with reduced-calorie diet (~500 kcal/day deficit) and regular physical activity. Nutrition and fitness consultation recommended.
- **Alcohol**: Caution advised; may impair glucose regulation and worsen GI symptoms in some users.
- **Pregnancy**: Liraglutide is not studied in pregnancy and should be discontinued ≥2 months before attempting conception. If pregnancy is confirmed, consult prescriber immediately.
- **Fertility**: Limited data on effects on ovulation or male fertility. Discuss family-planning intentions with prescriber.
- **Going on/off**: Do not stop abruptly without medical guidance. Gradual de-escalation is sometimes recommended. If restarting after a gap, re-titration from 0.6 mg is advised.
- **Concurrent GI medications**: If using Saxenda alongside metformin, acarbose, or other GI-active agents, monitor for compounded effects and consult prescriber.
- **Common OTC drugs**: Most OTC medications are compatible; antacids, H2-blockers, or proton-pump inhibitors may affect liraglutide or concurrent medication absorption. Consult pharmacist if long-term use is necessary.
