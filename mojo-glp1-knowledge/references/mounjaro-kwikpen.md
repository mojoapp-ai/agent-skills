# Mounjaro (猛健樂) KwikPen Knowledge Base

> Note: This document summarises the dose form, use, and storage of the KwikPen. **It is reference material only, not medical advice.** Any decision involving dose adjustments, injection technique, storage, or side-effect management should be confirmed with a prescribing physician or pharmacist.

Sources: Lilly DailyMed Prescribing Information, emc UK Patient Information Leaflet, Medical News Today.

---

## Core facts

- In community discussions, the figure "10 mg" almost always refers to **the once-weekly single dose**, not the total amount of drug in the pen.
- **Mounjaro KwikPen is a multiple-dose pre-filled pen** (多劑量預充筆 / 多剂量预充笔). Each pen delivers **4 doses** of **0.6 mL** each.
- Example: **10 mg KwikPen**
  - Per-injection dose: **10 mg / 0.6 mL**
  - Total pen volume: **2.4 mL**
  - Doses per pen: **4 × 10 mg**
  - Total drug per pen: **40 mg / 2.4 mL**
- A new pen needle is required for every injection.
- After the fourth dose, a small amount of fluid remains in the pen; the manufacturer label says "do not use it."

The active ingredient is **tirzepatide (替爾泊肽 / 替尔泊肽)**, a dual GLP-1 / GIP receptor agonist administered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection (皮下注射).

## Common community misconceptions

### Misconception 1: "10 mg" means the pen contains 10 mg in total

**Wrong.** A 10 mg KwikPen delivers 10 mg *per injection*. Because the pen holds 4 doses, the total drug content is actually **40 mg**.

### Misconception 2: "If I dial down a 10 mg pen to 5 mg per shot, I can get two more shots out of it"

This conflates the *single-dose label* with the *total pen content*.

- The official 10 mg KwikPen = 10 mg per dose, 4 doses per pen.
- Community discussion of "dialing smaller increments / stretching the pen / splitting doses" is non-manufacturer practice.
- "10 mg / 0.6 mL" is the per-dose strength, not the total pen content.

### Misconception 3: "KwikPen is a single-shot disposable pen"

**No.** Mounjaro KwikPen is a multiple-dose pre-filled pen:

- One pen delivers 4 doses.
- **Pen needles (針頭 / 针头) are single-use** — change the needle for every injection.
- **The pen itself is never shared between patients**, even with a new needle (infection-transmission risk).

## Use and storage essentials

- Dosing frequency: **once weekly**.
- Common starting regimen: **2.5 mg once weekly for 4 weeks**, then up-titrate to 5 mg; further increments of 2.5 mg per step at the prescribing physician's discretion.
- Injection sites: abdomen (腹部), thigh (大腿); upper outer arm (上臂後側) if administered by another person.
- Rotate injection sites between doses.
- Use a new needle every time.
- **Never share a pen**, even with a fresh needle.
- After opening: Lilly recommends using the pen within 30 days.
- After the fourth dose, the pen must be discarded properly per manufacturer instructions.

## Residual medication (殘劑 / 残留药液)

### The facts

- The KwikPen is designed for 4 doses, but a noticeable amount of fluid remains after the fourth dose — community reports suggest the leftover volume can approach a full dose.
- The residual is included by design for *priming* purposes; it is not a defect or "incomplete fill."
- **Lilly's official instruction is explicit: do not use the leftover.** ("Do not transfer the leftover medicine from your Pen into a syringe.")
- A large body of community discussion (notably r/Mounjaro under the label "golden dose") describes off-label extraction practices. Any attempt to extract and use residual medication **departs from the manufacturer's instructions; the user assumes the associated risks.**

### Why turning the dial harder does not work

After the fourth dose, the KwikPen mechanically locks. Forcing the dial does **not produce a controllable dose** — the actual amount delivered is unpredictable.

### Concentration table per pen strength

For anyone discussing residual-volume to mg conversions, the underlying concentrations are:

| Pen strength | Concentration |
|---|---:|
| 2.5 mg KwikPen | 4.17 mg/mL |
| 5 mg KwikPen | 8.33 mg/mL |
| 7.5 mg KwikPen | 12.5 mg/mL |
| 10 mg KwikPen | 16.7 mg/mL |
| 12.5 mg KwikPen | 20.8 mg/mL |
| 15 mg KwikPen | 25 mg/mL |

### Risk reminders

- Any procedure that breaches the pen housing can introduce bacteria; **infection and abscess risk** is real.
- Lilly's 30-day post-opening shelf life applies to the four-dose regimen; it is not a safety window for extracted residual.
- Mixing residual fluid from multiple pens is not advisable.
- Medical News Today (2025) reports that Lilly is reportedly redesigning the pen to eliminate the residual volume.

### What only a clinician should decide

- Whether to use residual medication at all.
- The specific extraction procedure (this is a medical operation, not a community how-to).
- Any dose adjustment.

An LLM should not provide a step-by-step extraction tutorial — that is a clinical procedure best taught by a physician or pharmacist.
