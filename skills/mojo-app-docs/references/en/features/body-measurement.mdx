# Body Measurements

mojo tracks the following body metrics to help you understand how your body changes throughout your weight loss journey.

## What Gets Tracked

**Primary Measurements:**
- **Weight** (kg / lb)
- **Body Fat %** (%)
- **Fat Mass** (kg / lb) — can be logged independently or calculated automatically from weight × body fat %
- **Muscle Mass** (kg / lb) — skeletal muscle weight

**Specialized Measurements (Premium):**
- **Visceral Fat Level** (1–59)
- **Waist Circumference** (cm / in)

## Visceral Fat Levels Explained

mojo categorizes the visceral fat level (1–59) you log into three tiers:

| Tier | Range |
|------|-------|
| Normal | 1–9 |
| Slightly High | 10–14 |
| High | 15–59 |

> Visceral fat level is a measurement provided by body composition scales (e.g., TANITA, Omron). Different brands may use different grading thresholds; mojo uses the tiers above as a reference.

## How to Log Data

### Auto-Sync (Recommended)

Use a smart scale that supports Apple Health / Health Connect. After stepping on the scale, your data syncs to mojo automatically.

What can sync automatically:
- Weight and body fat % (iOS + Android)
- Waist circumference (iOS only, via Apple Health)

### Manual Entry

Go to Settings → Data Management → Body Measurements and tap the "+" button in the top right.

The following can only be entered manually for now:
- Muscle mass (Apple Health and Health Connect don't support skeletal muscle mass)
- Visceral fat level
- Waist circumference (Android)

## Progress Charts

View trend charts in the Progress tab:
- Weight, body fat, and muscle mass trends over time
- Time range options: 1 month / 3 months / 6 months / All
- Tap a data point to see the exact value
- Automatically displays in your preferred unit (kg/lb)

Charts use injection dates as the X-axis reference, pulling measurements recorded within 3 days before or after each injection. Building a habit of weighing yourself before each injection will give you more complete chart data.
