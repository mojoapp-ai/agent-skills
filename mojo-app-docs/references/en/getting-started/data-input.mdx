# Data Input

> Not sure where to log your data? Here's a guide to inputting different types of data in mojo

mojo supports multiple types of health data, and each type has its own input method. Some data can be automatically synced from Apple Health (iOS) or Health Connect (Android), while others require manual entry.

---

## Body Measurements

### Weight

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Health platform sync**: Automatically read from Apple Health / Health Connect
- ✅ **Manual input**: Tap the **hollow circle** in the trend chart on the Today tab

**Steps:**
1. Open the Today tab
2. Find the weight trend chart
3. Tap a hollow circle on the chart
4. Enter your weight and save

---

### Body Fat Percentage

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Health platform sync**: Synced from smart scales that support body fat measurement
- ✅ **Manual input**: Tap the **hollow circle** in the trend chart on the Today tab

---

### Muscle Mass

**Input Methods:**
- ❌ **Auto-sync not supported**: mojo does not currently support auto-syncing muscle mass from health platforms
- ✅ **Manual input**: Tap the **hollow circle** in the trend chart on the Today tab

**Note:** Muscle mass typically requires a body composition analyzer with InBody or similar technology.

---

## GLP-1 Records

### Injection Logs

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Manual input**: Enter in the injection progress section on the Today tab

**Steps:**
1. Open the Today tab
2. Find the **injection progress section** above the chart
3. Tap the section
4. Enter the injection date, dosage, and other details

---

### Side Effects

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Manual input**: Enter in the injection progress section on the Today tab

---

## Lifestyle Data

### Food Logs

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Health platform sync**: Sync via third-party food tracking apps
- ✅ **Deep link import**: Import meals directly from supported apps into mojo
- ✅ **Manual input**: Tap "+" in the nutrition section on the Today tab

**Recommended apps:**
- MyFitnessPal
- FatSecret
- Lose It!

---

### Exercise Records

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Health platform sync only**
- ❌ **Manual input not supported**

**How to log exercise:**
- Use an Apple Watch or Garmin watch to record workouts
- Use the iPhone's built-in "Fitness" app
- Use exercise apps that support Apple Health / Health Connect (e.g. Strava, Nike Run Club)

---

### Sleep Records

**Input Methods:**
- ✅ **Health platform sync only**
- ❌ **Manual input not supported**

**How to log sleep:**
- Use the sleep tracking feature on Apple Watch or a Garmin watch
- Use sleep tracking apps that support Apple Health / Health Connect (e.g. AutoSleep, Sleep Cycle)

---

### Water Intake

**Input methods:**
- **Manual input** in the water section on the Today tab:
  - Tap **+** / **−** to add or remove a serving at a time
  - **Long-press the progress bar and drag** to set an exact amount (haptic feedback every 50 ml)
- **Health platform sync**: Automatically read from Apple Health / Health Connect

**Features:**
- Set a custom daily hydration goal
- Supports ml / oz units

## Setting Up Health Data Sync

To let mojo automatically read your health data:

1. Open the Settings tab
2. Find the health data sync section
3. Turn on the sync toggle
4. Authorize mojo to access the relevant health data
5. Return to the Today tab and pull down to refresh

## FAQ

### Why can't I find where to log food?

Tap "+" in the nutrition section on the Today tab to manually add a meal. You can also use a third-party food tracking app that supports health platform sync (e.g. MyFitnessPal) — data will automatically sync to mojo.

### Where are the hollow circles?

In the trend charts on the Today tab, any date without data shows as a hollow circle. Dates with data show as filled circles. Tap a hollow circle to enter data for that date.

### Where is the injection progress section?

On the Today tab, there is an injection progress section **above** the trend charts. Tap it to log injections and side effects.

### Can I bulk-import data?

Yes. In the "Data Management" section of the Settings tab, you can use the CSV bulk import feature (currently iOS only). This is useful for importing large amounts of historical data from other apps or Excel.
